cadence shift

After 10 years of monthly releases, VS Code moved to weekly releases in February.

AI was moving faster than monthly releases.
Users expected model-speed improvements.
Smaller batches were safer to ship.
Land the cadence change: the old monthly release muscle was strong, but AI work needed a faster loop for learning, correction, and confidence.

section 02

When building got faster, alignment became the bottleneck.

AI meant more human collaboration, not less: tighter workstreams, visible DRIs, and weekly decision loops.

Start the section with the counterintuitive point: AI did not remove the need for human alignment. It made alignment more important because the team could pursue more possible work.

We moved toward faster alignment loops instead of heavier plans.

  • Temporary workstreams form around the highest-leverage outcomes.
  • DRIs make ownership explicit while preserving broad collaboration.
  • Weekly goals force tradeoffs while the facts are still fresh.

AI made building faster, so humans had to align more often.

before
  • Teams planned by areaWork followed long-lived ownership boundaries.
  • Weekly planning set the frameA planning call plus daily team standups was enough when the work moved in longer arcs.
  • Handoffs carried contextProduct, design, and engineering could sequence decisions more cleanly.
now
  • Temporary workstreams form around outcomesPeople swarm the highest-leverage AI opportunities, then reset.
  • DRIs make decisions visibleA directly responsible individual keeps ownership clear as implementation fans out.
  • Workstreams meet dailySome streams needed twice-daily standups to keep decisions, validation, and release risk moving together.
Land the collaboration point explicitly: AI did not reduce human coordination. It increased the premium on shared judgment, visible ownership, and faster alignment loops.

Stanza 5

To succeed with AI, evolve the process around the tool.

  • Raise alignment frequency when implementation gets cheaper.
  • Use temporary workstreams and DRIs to focus high-velocity teams.
  • Invest in issue intelligence, PR pipeline, and observability as first-class product infrastructure.
  • Let roles evolve toward judgment, prototypes, and direct contribution.
next-steps.mdaudience playbook
1. Pick one workflow where AI changed throughput. 2. Identify the new bottleneck: alignment, review, or feedback. 3. Add a weekly operating rhythm around it. 4. Instrument quality before scaling volume. 5. Redefine roles around judgment, not tasks.
